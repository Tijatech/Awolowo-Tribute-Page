Chief Jeremiah Obafemi Awolowo

The man who contributed immensively to Nigeria Education and Political system

Here's a time line of Cheif. Awolowo's life:

  • 1909 - Born in Ikenne, Ogun State
  • 1927 - He worked as an assistant teacher before attending Wesley College, Ibadan, to undergo training as an educator, graduating in 1927.
  • 1932- He took up a clerical post at the College.
  • 1934 - He started a trading business working for the Motor Transporter and Produce Trader, and began to write newspaper articles. He founded the Nigerian Produce Traders Association and edited the Nigerian Worker. He also became secretary of the Nigerian Motor Transport Union.
  • 1937 - He married to Hannah Idowu Dideolu and they had two sons and three daughters. He organized a successful strike against an "unjust and inequitable colonial law".
  • 1940- He was an active member of NYM (Nigerian Youth Movement) and became Ibadan branch secretary.
  • 1942- He led agitation that resulted in the reform of the Ibadan Native Authority Advisory Board.
  • 1943- He co-founded the Trades Union Congress.
  • 1944- He organized a mass protest against the ban on exporting palm kernel. It was this grass-level activism that did much to convince ordinary people that they could take on the British, and win. He enrolled at the University of London as an External Student and graduated with the degree of Bachelor of Commerce (Hons.).
  • 1945 - He wrote his first book, Path to Nigerian Freedom, in which he was highly critized the British policies of indirect administration and called for rapid moves toward self-government and Africanization of administrative posts in Nigeria. While in London, he helped to found the Egbe Omo Oduduwa (Society of the Descendants of Oduduwa, the mythical ancestor of the Yoruba-speaking peoples), an organization devoted to the study and preservation of Yoruba culture.
  • 1946 - He was called to the Bar by the Honorable Society of the Inner Temple.
  • 1947 - He developed a thriving practice as a barrister in Ibadan. He advocated federalism as the only basis for equitable national integration and, as head of the Action Group.
  • 1949 - He founded the Nigerian Tribune, a private Nigerian newspaper, which he used to spread nationalist consciousness among Nigerians
  • 1950 He organized the Action Group (political party) which requested for the end of British domination in Nigeria.
  • 1951-He was a solicitor and advocate of the Superior Court of Nigeria.
  • 1952- He traveled extensively, visiting Egypt, Ceylon, Pakistan, and India discussing self-determination and anti-colonial struggle. An admirer of Jawaharlal Nehru, he later serialized his autobiography in The Tribune.
  • 1954 - He became the first premier of the Western Region, on which occasion he was awarded an honorary chieftaincy. During his tenure as leader and premier, he held the regional ministerial portfolios of local government, finance, and economic planning. He was also chairman of the Regional Economic Planning Commission.
  • 1958- He took part in Constitutional talks in London and Ghana. During this period he also visited the United States, Germany, Italy, and Japan to encourage trade relations.
  • 1959 - Controversially, and at considerable expense, he introduced free primary education for all and free health care for children in the Western Region, established the first television service in Africa, and the Oduduwa Group, all of which were financed from the highly lucrative cocoa industry which was the mainstay of the regional economy. On the eve of independence, he resigned from the Premiership so that he could run for the federal assembly.
  • 1962 - He was charged for conspiring with some Ghanaians under Kwame Nkrumah to overthrow the federal government. He was sentenced to a ten year term.
  • 1966 - In his book, Thoughts on the Nigerian Constitution, he defended federalism but recommended the creation of 18 smaller states to replace the regions.
  • 1971 - He resigned to protest the government's continuation of military rule.
  • 1983 - He ran for president as the candidate for the Unity Party of Nigeria, losing to Shehu Shagari.
